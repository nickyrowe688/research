# Bos-derived verbs

## Godhvos

Verbal noun

- Understand
  - skant denveeth buz ore guthvaz ha clappia Sousenack (NB) "hardly anybody but is able to understand and speak English"

Present

- Ability
  - N'ora vî skrefa na mụî (L) "I can write no more"
  - Mî or môs (L) "I can go"
  - Ol an boble en Poreea ne mi or dha ganzingy (NB) "Neither all the people in St Ives nor I can catch"
  - skant denveeth buz ore guthvaz ha clappia Sousenack (NB) "hardly anybody but is able to understand and speak English"
- Knowledge (fact)
  - Na ore den veeth (NB) "Nobody knows"
  - Me a oare hemma (NB) "I know this"
  - Na oravee drel an Kembreean gweel (NB) "I do not know what the Welsh can do"
  - Mî a ụôr (L) "I know"
  - An Arleth why ore ewe Deawe seere louare (TB) "The Lord ye know is God sure enough"
  - Me ore gwir (JT) "I know well"
  - Me a woer ny wrug an beys tha voos qwicke destryes (2382) "I know he did not make the world to be so quickly destroyed"
  - Why a wore ynta henna (121) "You know that very well"
  - Henna ty a wore ynta (1145) "You know that very well"
  - Mara custa (2332) "If you know"
  - Na oren pendra tho weel anotha (NB) "We do not know what to make of it"
  - tho an jorna ma tegge na orren panna letherau (NB) "to this day bearing we know not what letters"
  - leb ma leverres gennam a orama dro tho an tavaz Curnooack (NB) "where I have said what I know about the Cornish language"
  - Dew ewyr (L) "God knows"
  - Deew a ore (R) "God knows"
- Know (a person)
  - hi ụor neb dên (L) "she knows some man"
- Know of (be aware of)
  - deu po try a orama anetha (NB) "two or three that I know of"

Imperfect

- Knew (a fact)
  - an gie oyah tel eran'gye en noath (R) "They knew that they were naked"
  - Me a ụeyan por-dhâ tro nag ô e gerlevran Kembrîan (L) "I knew very well that it was not a Welsh glossary"
  - eue levarraz droua 'gever' ul eve a wya dro 'gevern' (NB) "he said that 'goat' was all he knew about 'gevern'
- Ability
  - Me ny wothyan gwyll dotha (1015) "I did not know how to deal with him"
  - Ny wothyan tabm y naha (775) "I could not in any way refuse him"

Conditional

- would know (a fact)
  - Maga fure te a vea avel Dew, hag a uffya pub tra (597) "You would be as wise as God, and would know all things"

Past subjunctive

- knew (a fact)
  - A cuffan y voza gwyre (673) "If I knew it to be true"
