Modal verbs

They became modal because the previous distinctions of usage broke down in favour of a new system closer to English, in which choice of modal was much freer.

gallos

No past/present participle, no imperative. Not used with auxiliaries gwul/bos.

Present:

  • Used to express ability

    • dho rei an gụella dyskụedhyans a ellam (L) "to give the best explanation I can"
    • Me ellam (P) "I can"
    • Na ellam (Gw) "I cannot"

  • Used with a verbal noun to express ability of the verbal noun

    • na oravee drel an Kembreean gweel rag tho gwetha ge tavaz (NB) "I do not know what the Welsh can do to save their language"
    • Whi el dendal gose bounans obba (NB) "You can earn your living here"
    • Na ellam ry dotha mouy tre guffia them (NB) "I cannot accord it more than I should"
    • neb ma na ell o perthe ve (TB) "those that cannot abide me"
    • Ni a ell debre athore oll an gweth an loar (R) "We can eat of all the trees in the garden"
    • hag a ellen kavas mez (K) "that we can devise"
    • Ny yll ny buz gawas bonogath da (K) "We can only find goodwill"
    • gy el e glowas (TT) "they can hear him"
    • N'ell e kleụez vyth (L) "He cannot hear at all"
    • Elo why clapier Kernuack? (P) "Can you speak Cornish?"
    • Ne ell e ge debre (L) "He cannot eat them"
    • ny yll bos (1104) "it cannot be so"
    • Nyedge in ker lemmyn ha myer terathe mar kyll bos kevys (2452) "Fly away now and see whether land can be found"
    • Me as danven pur uskys, sight an noer mar kill gwelas (2455) "I will send her right speedily to see whether any sight of dry land she can see"

Conditional:

  • Might:

    • An Sousen metessen olga gawaz maga nebbaz skeeanz vel an Brittez (NB) "Perhaps the English might have had as little skill as the British"
    • Radden olga bose parrez tho laverol (NB) "Some might be prepared to say"

  • Should:

    • Mî a ụon pordha trẏ kaldzha ụẏnnyn a ôr koụz Kernûak deal an lether-ma (L) "I know very well that one who knows Cornish should be able to fully understand this letter"

  • Conditional (could, could have)

    • ol an drokter alga bos kevys (K) "all imaginable disadvantages"
    • Mî a ụon pordha trẏ kaldzha trigerio an dheaụ ụlaz-ma gîl an ober-ma pẏlta gụel tro yụ gụreiz geno vî (L) "I know very well that the inhabitants of these two countries could perform this task much better than it has been done by me"
    • Mî a ụon pordha trẏ kaldzha ụẏnnyn tibiaz... (L) "I know very well that one could imagine..."

  • Preterite (could, were able to)

    • e na allja (TT) "he could not (stop him - Menja tus Kernowek)"
    • na aldzha an Zoụzon y gorra emêz a'n Kar-êsk byz an vledhan 936 (L) "the Saxons could not expel them from Exeter until the year 936"

  • Imperfect (could, were able to)

    • Na algama e clappia (NB) "I could not speak it"
    • Na algia ea clappia na screffa Curnoack pecarra why (OP) "He could not speak or write Cornish like you"
    • dho skrefa ketella kaldzha vî (L) "to write, so as I could"

  • Subjunctive (might)

    • malga boaz composez a ve cowsez gen Arleth neue der an prophet (R) "that it might be fulfilled that was spoken by the Lord of heaven through the prophet"
    • malga e boaz composez (R) "that it might be fulfilled"
    • malga da dethow booze heer (R) "that thy days might be long"
    • malgama geel (Gw) "that I might do"
    • maldzha va prêv erra e wrêg guitha kẏmpez... (L/NB) "so that he might prove whether his wife was keeping faith"
    • traldzhan deska dhort i gilez (L) "so that they might learn from one another"
    • trẏ kaldzha an hụêl bohodzhak-ma pîga erel am'an dho dallah ẏdn da (L) "that this poor work might encourage others to begin a good one"

Subjunctive:

  • Present subjunctive only (imperfect subjunctive replaced by conditional)
    • mala ve moaze ha gortha thotha aweeth (R) "so that I may go and worship Him also"
    • mol de dethyo boz pel (TB) "that thy days may be long"
    • mal nagwonnen moaz gwadn trea (Gw) "so that no-one shall go home hungry"
    • mollough (B) "so that ye may"

gwul

A primary auxiliary verb, but it has modal uses

mednes

Present:

  • Prediction:
    • Mi a vedn krêg ragta (L/NB) "I will hang for it"
    • Na eue liklaud dre vidnava treegaz pel hep merwal akar (NB) "It is not likely that it will live long without dying out"
    • Eue a dael bose Ankou e hunnen a vedden pedeere drerama creege hedna tho bose gweer (NB) "It must be Death himself who will think that I believe it to be true"
    • E vedn ri hanou da tha muzzi ol (JB) "it will give all girls a good reputation"
    • Dewe e vedd'n e worras... (JT) "God will help him"
    • Gwella ghy vedn wharra (JB) "they will sell better"
    • Na vedn an tavaz ma beska boz kavas arta (JB) "This language will never be found again"
    • Ancho vedn gweel an dean euhella cotha dadn daor (JB) "Death will cause the most exalted man to fall beneath the ground"
    • Puha vedn kavas an gwel skians ol (JB) "He will have the best knowledge of all"
    • An hern gwave vedn geele droag d'an hern have (OP) "The winter pilchards will adversely affect the summer pilchards"
    • Fleaz hep skeeanz vedn gweel gu seeanz (J) "Children without sense will follow their fancy"
    • Why vedn kelly meare a peath (J) "You will lose a lot of wealth"
    • E vedn gẏz gîl sâụ (L) "It will make you well"
    • Dew ewhella vedn ry peth yw gwella ol rag why (Gw) "God in the highest will grant what is best of all of you"
  • Prediction based on general experience:
    • Saime vedn cotha (JB) "fish oil will come out"
    • Eye vedn gwerras de gu teez (J) "They will help their menfolk"
    • Ne vedn nevera doos vâs a tavaz re hîr (Gw) "Good will never come of too long a tongue"
    • Hedna vedn gwitha corf en ehaz (Gw) "That will keep one's body healthy"
    • ha e weetha eve a vedd'n (JT) "and keep it he will (the crown)"
  • Shall:
    • lebben dru a devethez ita Brez, mea vedn gweel Duath an skreefma durt... (NB) "seeing it is now come into my Mind, I will conclude this scribble..."
    • Mee a ved'n moze da whelaz weale da weele (NB) "I will go to seek work"
    • Mee a ved'n laverel deez (NB) "I will tell you"
    • Mî vedn mɐz alêz (L) "I shall go abroad"
    • Ha enna ni a ved'n e ara (TT) "And we shall leave him there"
  • Willingness or intention:
    • Ve de Arlith Deu vedne dry pehazo an tazo war an flehas... (TB) "I the Lord thy God will visit the sins of the fathers upon the children..."
    • Na vedn an Arlith gave do neb ra e henwelle heb oatham (TB) "The Lord will not forgive him who takes His name in vain"
    • Mor menta (Gw) "If thou wilt"
    • Me vedn kenz (L) "I prefer"
    • Mar menta gụellaz an ôst an tshei... (L/NB) "If you wish to see the host..."
    • Mor minta poz cotha an doer... (R) "If you will only bow down..."
    • Me a venja moaz tre mor menta (P) "I would like to go home if you will"
    • a mennas (P) "if you wish"
    • Oll a rimah ve vedn ry theeze (R) "All these will I give you"
    • Herod vedn whelaz an flô younk (R) "Herod will seek the young child"
    • Me vedn meare cressha tha dewhan (R) "I will greatly increase thy sorrow"
    • Na vedn an Arleth gon cawas en Paraves rag komeras e hanow en vaine (R) "The Lord will not receive us into Paradise for taking his name in vain"
    • Peege gungans mee a vedd'n (TT) "I will pray for them"
    • Me veden e thone (T) "I will bear it"
    • Pew veda why gowas rag seera rag as flo? (T) "Who will you get for a father for your child?"
    • Pendre veda why geal rag lednow rag as flo? (T) "What will you do for clothes for your child?"
    • Mi vedn gẏz pardẏna hụei (L) "I will pardon you"
    • Mi vedn gẏz gụelaz arta gẏdhihụar (L) "I will see you again this evening"
    • Trẏ vedoụ hụei kemerez? (L) "What will you take?"
    • Grew, mena why (T) "Do so, if you wish"
    • Na vadam (Gw) "I will not"
    • Meea na vidna cowz a Sawznack (RC) "I will not speak English"
    • Mor mennow dereval worbidn an pow yeine (J) "If you want to build against the cold"
  • Unknown:
    • Idn vedn ry leeaz (Gw) "One will produce many" (*** SGMC58)
    • Y vedn trehe gyz brandzha (B) "They will cut your throat" (*** SGMC58)
    • Me vedn boz dyliez (B) "I shall be revenged" (*** SGMC58)
    • Me vedn gusendzhi (B) "I'll bet" (*** SGMC58)
    • tre vedn a dama r'hei (B) "that my mother will give" (*** SGMC58)

Conditional

  • Willingness/wish (imperfect)
    • E na vengha servia na velha (NB) "He did not wish to serve any longer"
    • E vengha moas teua [trea] tha e wreag (NB) "He wanted to go home to his wife"
    • An vartshants a vendzha arta dhɐ Dzhûan mɐz drê barh an dzhei (L/NB) "The merchants again wanted John to go back with them"
    • na venga hye boaze comfortyez (R) "she would not be comforted"
  • Wish (conditional)
    • Me a venja moaz tree (Gw) "I would like to go home"
    • Me vendzha kenz (B) "I would rather"
  • Conditional
    • ev a venya kavaz fraga e ouna en skreefa composter (NB) "he would find reason to correct it in its orthography"
    • Ni venja pea a munna (JT) "We would pay the money"
    • Me a venja cowas na peath (JT) "I would find something"
    • Na venja hedda whath gun greevia (JT) "That would not trouble us any more"
    • Karendzhia vendzhia, kavaethiaz na vendzhia (Gw) "Love would, self-interest would not"
    • Mendzha vî po ny vendzham (L) "Whether I would or no"
  • Perfect conditional
    • An gwase a vynsa leskye agen esowe (1128) "The fellow would have burned our grain"
  • Exhortation (would that..., if only)
    • Menja tiz Kernuak buz gasowaz (JT) "If only Cornish people would listen"
    • menja e buz gweel dua (JT) "If only he would make an end"
  • Thought he could (he heard = imperfect?)
    • ev a vendzha kloụaz dhên aral en gụili (L/NB) "he thought he could hear another man in bed"
